# Vizcom at Dutch Design Week 2026: What we're bringing

![Vizcom booth M-114, Material Futures hall, Dutch Design Week 2026](samples/renders/ddw_2026_booth.png)

Dutch Design Week runs October 19–27, 2026 at Klokgebouw in Strijp-S, Eindhoven — nine days, around 350,000 visitors, the largest design event in Northern Europe. We'll be there for the full run, in the Material Futures hall at booth M-114.

This is our first year on the floor at DDW. The brief we set ourselves was small and concrete: bring the tools, bring the people who use them, and answer real questions in real time. No keynote. No glass case. The booth is set up for working sessions — sit down, sketch something, push it through Vizcom, walk out with a render you made yourself.

## What we're showing

Daily live demos at the booth, 14:00 and 17:00 CET. Sophia Silver from our product team is leading them. Each demo runs about 25 minutes and takes a sketch through render, into 3D, and back to a presentation-ready image — the same path our designers use to compress days of iteration into an afternoon.

We'll also be set up to run one-on-one sessions throughout the day. If you're at DDW with a specific project — a soft-good, a piece of hardware, a vehicle interior, anything you're working through — bring it. We'd rather look at your work than show you ours.

The booth itself is in the Material Futures hall because that's where the conversations we care about happen: how a render becomes a real object, what a designer keeps in their hands when AI is in the loop, where material choice still has to be human.

## Who's speaking

On Wednesday October 22 at 16:00 CET we're running a panel called *AI in industrial design — five practitioners, no slides.* The title is the format. Five working industrial designers, one moderator, no decks, no demos — just a conversation about what's actually changed in the last eighteen months and what hasn't.

Emil Lukas from Vizcom is moderating. Two panelists are confirmed:

- **Julius** (julius.works, Berlin) — designer behind The Exhaust Light, a tealight holder rendered in Vizcom and fabricated from stainless steel exhaust piping.
- **Ádám Miklósi** (Budapest) — industrial designer behind Fabracers, a series of collectible derby cars built from extruded aluminum, cork, and solid birch.
