# 1 million designers, six months, and the line that kept going

*By Jordan Taylor, CEO and co-founder — published May 20, 2026*

![One million designers on Vizcom](samples/renders/one_million_designers_hero.png)

What if the gap between an idea and a thing you could hold was measured in minutes, not weeks? What if a sketchbook never had to be the last place an idea lived? What if a million designers, in 142 countries, were already working that way?

**As of this week, 1 million designers use Vizcom.** Up from 700,000 when we announced our Series B in October. Six months. 300,000 designers. 1,247,000 minutes spent designing in Vizcom — more in six months than in all of 2025.

## Where this started

It started as a personal frustration. I was a designer at Honda. The tools were getting in the way — not of the rendering, but of the thinking that happens *during* the rendering. Most of the loops a designer wants to run never happen, because the next version costs another hour, and the hour costs a meeting, and the meeting costs the energy you needed to chase the version after that.

Vizcom started as a question I asked myself in that loop: what if the middle phase — the messy middle, the part between the line and the object — was no longer where ideas went to die?

Six months ago we said we wanted to make the line go further. The line has gone further than any of us predicted, and it's because of who picked it up.

## What you built

The numbers are real, but they aren't the story. The story is in the renders.

It's in the **concrete keyboard** Emil cast by hand after art-directing the look in Vizcom. It's in **The Saman**, the mule Andrew Moya and Juan Rujana drew from Pre-Columbian gold and put into production with Zellerfeld. It's in **Liven**, Otto Loikkanen's sunrise alarm clock, born during Finland's darkest season and ending up on a real bedside table. It's in **Catapill** and **Digits** — biomimetic and sculptural mules from designers in two different countries, both arriving at the same conclusion: the idea didn't have to die in a sketchbook this time.

A few of the things you made, in six months:

- **1,247,000 minutes** designing in Vizcom — almost double all of 2025 in half the time.
- **168 Fortune 500 customers**, up from 150 in October. Honda, Ford, Stellantis, Sonos, New Balance, Estée Lauder, Gulfstream, Target, Dell, Omega. The teams who build the objects in your kitchen, your driveway, your closet, your cockpit.
- **142 countries** represented in the active designer base.
- **11 Vizcom Worlds events**, ~4,200 designers in the room. Squirrels on scooters. Helicopters chasing dragons. A car engineered to disrupt Minecraft creepers. (You know who you are.)

You can copy features. You cannot copy a million designers showing up to share what they're working on.

## What's next

We're not chasing more features for their own sake. We're going deeper — into the middle phase of design, the part where most of the decisions actually happen.

Think of it like autonomous vehicles. You still set the destination. The system helps you get there. The designer is the orchestrator; Vizcom is the road, the maps, the hands on the wheel when you want them and out of the way when you don't. The next year of work is about widening that road and shortening the trip, in the core domains where you've told us it matters most — automotive, footwear, apparel, consumer goods.

We're hiring across engineering, design, and beyond. The next million designers are already showing up — in our discord, in our community events, in the renders we see in our feed every morning. We want to build for them too.

## The bigger picture

AI will not replace designers. It will amplify them. We've said that since Series B and a million people have now spent 1.2 million minutes making it true.

A million designers is a number. What it means is that a million people pulled an idea further than they could have alone, and decided to keep pulling.
