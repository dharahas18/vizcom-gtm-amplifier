Hiroshi Tanaka, senior designer on Honda's interior-design team in Tokyo, has spent the last nine months using Vizcom on the 2027 Civic interior redesign — taking concepts from sketch to executive review in two days instead of seven.

Studio for the first pass — full-bleed canvas, proportions on the page before any decision about what kind of render is coming next. Modify for material — more than thirty interior fabric combinations tested without re-prompting, without leaving the canvas. Animate for stakeholder walkthroughs, replacing a three-week pre-visualization cycle the team used to send out before every executive review.

The number that matters is not thirty. The number that matters is that a question raised in the morning meeting can be answered, visually, in the afternoon — by the designer who raised it.

"Vizcom collapses the gap between sketch and review. We're presenting to the executive design team with renders that used to take a week. The decision velocity has changed how we work." — Hiroshi Tanaka, Senior Designer, Honda
