# Meet Smart Dropper: A reference becomes a style

A reference says more than a prompt. Smart Dropper lets you point Vizcom at any image — a retro Mac, a tamagotchi, your favorite album cover — and pull the visual language straight into your design.

![Smart Dropper hero](samples/renders/smart_dropper_hero.png)

Reference images carry more information than a paragraph of prompt-writing can. A color palette. A surface treatment. The specific way light sits on plastic. The mood of an era. Most of that gets lost the moment you try to translate it into text. Smart Dropper skips the translation step.

## Why we built this

We've heard the same thing from you for months: the references on your moodboard already say what you want — you just need a way to get that into the render without re-prompting your way back to it. Designers shouldn't have to describe a tamagotchi when they can simply show one.

The friction was always the same. You'd find the perfect reference. You'd write three sentences trying to capture its essence. The render would land in the neighborhood but miss the specifics. You'd iterate. The reference itself — the thing you were chasing — was already on your screen. There was no clean way to drop it directly onto your design.

## How it works

### Point Smart Dropper at any image, get its style

Drop a reference into your Workbench, activate Smart Dropper, and target a sketch or render. The tool reads the reference's visual signature — material, color, lighting, era, texture — and applies it to your design while preserving the form you drew.

A retro Mac becomes the cue for off-white textured plastic and rounded CRT edges. A tamagotchi becomes the cue for late-90s consumer-electronic color and pixel-screen language. An album cover becomes the cue for a specific palette and grain.

### Keep your form, swap the vibe

The form you sketched stays intact. Smart Dropper changes how it reads, not what it is. That separation is the point — designers spend time getting proportions right, and a style transfer that overwrites geometry isn't useful.

You can apply Smart Dropper across multiple references on the same design and stack vibes together. Pull the color from one image, the surface from another, the mood from a third.

### Stay inside Workbench

Smart Dropper lives where you already work. No new view, no separate mode. The reference comes from the same library that holds your Collections, and the output lands on the same canvas where you'll keep iterating with Modify, Extract, and Instant Render.
