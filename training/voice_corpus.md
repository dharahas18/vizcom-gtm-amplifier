## Sample 1 — "Most Ideas Die in Sketchbooks"

**Channel:** vizcom.com/blog | **Date:** Apr 7, 2026 | **Author:** Emil Lukas (Program Manager, Advocates)
**Signal type:** UGC moment / employee creator story / techniques | **Hashtags:** None.

**Excerpt opening:** "There's a moment in every project where it stops being flexible. You've committed to materials. Tolerances. You're buying parts. And suddenly every decision has consequences — failure being one of them. I hit that moment building a concrete keyboard."

- **Topic:** Designer-first account of taking a concrete keyboard from sketch to build. Strongest example of modern Vizcom long-form voice.
- **Opener:** Cold open with tension moment — no nut graf. Drops you mid-stakes.
- **Structure:** Tension → context backstep → process → breakdown → recovery → art direction → reflection → soft CTA. ~1,400 words. 8 H2 subheads.
- **Cadence:** Short sentences. Fragments as paragraph beats ("It works. Exceptionally well."). Long sentences reserved for process detail. White space is a tool.
- **Vocabulary:** "see something clearly," "art directing, not designing," "push it further than you think you can." Specific materials (palm sander, steel rods, silicone). Single ➡️ in CTA.
- **Forbidden moves:** Don't invent failure moments — Emil's are real. Don't import this voice wholesale into product-update posts.

**Channel:** vizcom.com/blog | **Date:** Mar 31, 2026 | **Author:** Sophia Silver (PMM)
**Signal type:** Product launch (multi-feature) | **Hashtags:** None.

- **Forbidden moves:** No "revolutionary." Don't bury "available now" — modern Vizcom states it at the bottom.

## A. Voice (modern Vizcom, late-2025 → 2026)

- **Designer-first, tool-second.** In creator stories, designer's name in first 30 words. (Samples 1, 3, 4, 5.)
- **Specific over abstract.** Materials, tools, real designers, real geographies named precisely. (Samples 1, 3, 4, 5, 8.)
- **Calm confidence over hype.** No "revolutionary," "game-changing," "transform," "unlock," "empower," "leverage." (Samples 2, 8, 11.)
- **Honest about limits.** Sample 1 (concrete keyboard is heavy/uncomfortable). Sample 5 (Julius's failed iterations). Sample 8 (refuses to oversell a billing change).
- **Community as the irreducible asset.** Sample 7: "You can copy features, but you cannot copy community."
- **Plural "we" for company; first-person "I" for creator stories.** Voice persona shifts cleanly between Sophia/Kim (we), Jordan (founder I/we), Emil + featured designers (I).

## G. Do's and Don'ts (evidence-cited)

**Do:**
- Lead with the designer's name and a specific moment. (Sample 1: concrete keyboard; Sample 3: Finland's darkest season.)
- Show the friction in the user's own words. (Sample 2: "the render looks great, but now I need to keep working on it.")
- Name materials and tools precisely. (Sample 4: "extruded aluminum profile, cork cockpit, and solid birch wheels.")
- Use one-sentence paragraphs as punctuation. (Sample 1 throughout.)
- End with a soft CTA. (Samples 2, 3, 5, 8, 11 close with "Try Vizcom for Yourself →" or similar.)
- Use a credits footer for creator stories. (Samples 3, 4, 5.)
- For milestones, lead with gratitude before achievement. (Samples 6, 7.)

**Don't:**
- Invent designer quotes, project facts, customer names, materials, or dates.
- Use forbidden words (`core_rules.md` Section 2).
- Frame AI as a replacement.
- Stack 4+ hashtags in Vizcom's own voice (UGC pattern — Sample 15).
- Name direct competitors in Vizcom's own copy.
- Use Sample 10's 2024 mythic register except in a manifesto.
- Make a small change sound like a paradigm shift (Samples 8, 11 deliberately don't).
- Use bullet lists in a creator story — Vizcom proses them out.
