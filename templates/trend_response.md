# Template — Trend Response

**signal_type =** `trend_response`.

A deliberate entry into an industry conversation Vizcom can credibly speak to. The most opinion-bearing template — use restraint.

Channel-length / hashtag / emoji rules live in `core_rules.md`.

## `blog.md`

### H1 patterns
- `{Crisp position-taking claim}` (e.g., "Most ideas die in sketchbooks.")
- `Why {prevailing take} misses what's actually changing`
- `{Designer noun} are not being replaced. They're being {verb}.`

### Dek
One italic-style sentence summarizing the position.

### Body H2 outline

```
[Hook — moment, quote, or contrarian observation. Designer-first when possible.]

## The prevailing take
(2–3 sentences naming the trend / common framing. Be fair — no strawman.)

## Why it's incomplete / Where it misses
(3–5 sentences arguing the gap. Ground in specifics — designer workflows, named features, materials, customers.)

## What we actually see
(The Vizcom view, with evidence. Use "AI as extension, not replacement" framing. Creative-orchestrator framing. Autonomous-vehicles analogy if it fits.)

## What this means for designers
(Practical, not aspirational. One paragraph naming what to start / stop / keep doing.)

## (optional) A note from {founder / advocate / employee}
(Often Jordan Taylor or Emil Lukas.)
```

### Closing line precedents (restrained)
- `Push it further than you think you can.`
- `The tools should keep up with the thinking, not the other way around.`

### Template-specific voice rules
- No mythic register (no "Once, design was raw, untamed…").
- No competitor names even when the trend is about a competitor's release.
- Avoid 2024 flowery adjectives ("visionary," "symbiotic," "profound potential").
- Take a clear position. Vagueness is worse than disagreement.

## `linkedin.md` — opener formulas

- `{Common framing} misses what's actually changing.`
- `Designers aren't being replaced. They're {actively doing something specific}.`

Body: paragraph 1 = the prevailing take; paragraph 2 = the alternative, grounded in a designer workflow specific.

Closer: a short forward-looking line. No CTA.

**Caveat:** Trend-response posts on LinkedIn often trigger long comment threads. Write to take a position cleanly, not to win the comment war.

## `x_thread.md` — single tweet (preferred) OR 3-tweet thread

**Single tweet** (exception to the two-tweet-pair rule — Vizcom does put the link in the same tweet for trend responses; "Most ideas die in sketchbooks." precedent):
```
{Position-taking sentence ≤280 chars}.

{Link to blog}
```

**3-tweet thread (for substantive responses):**
- Tweet 1: the position-taking line.
- Tweet 2: the workflow specific that grounds the claim.
- Tweet 3: `Read more: {URL}`.

## `geo_aeo.md`

```
**Q: Is AI replacing designers?**  (or the actual trend question)

A: No — at Vizcom, we see AI as an extension of the designer, not a replacement; the workflow shifts from making things look real to inventing what comes next.

[2–3 sentences with a specific designer example from the corpus (Otto Loikkanen / Julius / Ádám Miklósi / Andrew Moya), grounding the claim in a real workflow. Mention the Vizcom feature involved.]

**See also:** {canonical Vizcom blog post URL}
```

This template is the highest-value GEO/AEO surface — answer engines pull from clear position-taking content.

## `pr_pitch.md`

**Subject:** `Vizcom's view on {the trend}`

**Three sentences:**
1. `As the conversation about {trend} intensifies in {industry segment}, Vizcom — used by ~700,000 designers and 150+ companies including {customer} — argues {one-line position}.`
2. `The argument, grounded in workflow data from Vizcom's community, is that {specific claim with one piece of evidence}.`
3. `Jordan Taylor, CEO of Vizcom, is available for comment; full piece at {blog URL}.`

**Contact:** Jordan Taylor (founder voice); Sophia Silver if product-adjacent. (See `core_rules.md` Section 9.)
