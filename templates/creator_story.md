## `blog.md`

### H1 patterns
- `Hold Your Ideas: {Project Name}`
- `Make It Real: {Project Name}, {Designer}'s {Form}`
- `{Bold Statement}` (e.g., "Most Ideas Die in Sketchbooks")

### Dek
`How {designer/team} used Vizcom to {specific verb} {project} from {first state} to {final state}.`

### Body H2 outline (5–7 sections)

```
## The Spark / The Inspiration / The Brief
(Cold-open with the moment or a contrarian frame. Designer name + city + handle/studio in first 50 words.)

## Rethinking [the niche] / The Form / Material Choices: [material]
(Concrete materials, named references, real cultural anchors. Em-dashes welcome.)

## {Designer}'s Vizcom Workflow
   ### H3 — Rapid detail exploration (or similar)
   ### H3 — Context and interaction imagery (or similar)

## From [Pixels / Vizcom] to [Object / Bedside Table / Aluminum / Concrete]

## A Note to Fellow Designers / Finding the Right Moment for AI

## (optional) The next idea / What's next
```

### Credits footer (always end with this)

```
**Design:** [Designer name] — [link / handle]
**Location:** [City, Country]
**Specialization:** [Industrial Design / Footwear / Product Design / etc.]
**Visualization:** Vizcom
**Materials:** [comma-separated]
```

### Closing CTA (after credits)
`Want to bring your own ideas to life at the speed of thought? [Try Vizcom for Yourself →](https://app.vizcom.com)`
Then social follows: Instagram / X / LinkedIn / Discord.

## `linkedin.md` — opener formulas

- `{Product} is a {form-factor} {one-line positioning}.`
- `{Designer}'s {Product} {one-line action verb}.`

Body: 2 short paragraphs separated by a blank line (designers/materials/inspiration; then workflow/capability).

Closer (standalone poetic line; Vizcom precedents):
- `A mule designed for any gathering place.` (Saman)
- `Build it once, use it across every project.` (Collections)

Media: one hero render, video, or carousel (+5 images common for designer features).

## `x_thread.md` — opener formulas

**Tweet 1 (story tweet):**
```
{Product} by {Designer}. A {form} {one-line positioning}. {2–4 fragment sentences with em-dashes — materials, inspiration, tone}. Designed in Vizcom, [in production with X / made real using {process}].
```
Tag designer (`@handle`).

**Tweet 2 (link tweet):**
```
Read the full story: {blog URL}
```
Or `Check out his full process:` / `See how it was made:`.

## Worked example: Saman shoe drop (signals/2026-05-08-saman-mule.md)

**Input** (paraphrased from real captured posts):

> Andrew Moya and Juan Rujana designed The Saman — a mule rooted in Latino culture and Pre-Columbian artifacts. Drew from indigenous art of Central and South America: gold figures, ceremonial objects, primordial forms. The Amber color echoes ancestral gold. The seashell shapes the shoe itself. Veins run through the surface like tree roots — carrying through to the sole. Made with Zellerfeld. Hero render at /renders/saman_hero.png.

- `blog.md` (~900 words): "Hold Your Ideas: The Saman" → The Spark → Material Honesty → Andrew and Juan's Vizcom Workflow (H3 rapid iteration / H3 cultural reference) → From Pixels to Production with Zellerfeld → A Note to Fellow Designers → credits footer.
