# Template — Milestone

**signal_type =** `milestone`.

Funding rounds, customer-count thresholds, anniversaries, team expansions. Register is founder-letter — personal, reflective, plural-pronoun "we" or first-person "I."

Channel-length / hashtag / emoji rules live in `core_rules.md`.

## `blog.md`

### H1 patterns
- `Announcing our {$X} Series {Letter}`
- `{Year} Year in Review`
- `Vizcom Raises {$X} to {do thing}`
- `{N} designers, {N} renders, one mission`

### Dek
One-paragraph fact summary: milestone + why + use of funds (or meaning).

### Body H2 outline

```
[Hook — three patterns Vizcom uses:]
[A — "What if" stack (Series B): "What if you could hold your design? What if the gap…"]
[B — memory of friction: "I was a designer at Honda. The tools were getting in the way."]
[C — calendar moment: "As {year} comes to a close, we're reflecting on..."]

[The hard fact — dollar amount, count, date — in bold.]

## Where this started
(Origin story. Personal. 2–3 paragraphs.)

## What we've built
(Product / trust / community. For funding: italic blockquote from an investor.)

## What's next
(Depth direction, not a feature roadmap. Autonomous-vehicles analogy if it fits: "You set the destination. The system helps you get there.")

## The bigger picture
(The "in 5 years, designers will…" framing. "AI will not replace designers. It will amplify them." if earned.)

## Thank you
(Community first. Investors for funding. Users for year-in-review. /careers link for hiring milestones.)
```

### Closing line precedents
- `Because in the end, every icon still starts as a line. Our job is to make that line go further.` (Series B)
- `Keep sketching, keep shipping, and keep finding new ways to surprise yourself.` (Year in Review)

### Template-specific voice rules
- Plural "we" throughout.
- Aphorisms allowed (this template is the most aphorism-tolerant).
- No "we're so excited" / "we're thrilled."

## `linkedin.md` — opener formulas

- Funding: `We've raised {$X} in Series {Letter} to {do the thing}.`
- Year-end: `{Number} {unit} this year. Here's what you built.`

Body: paragraph 1 = fact + gratitude; paragraph 2 = what the milestone enables (concrete, not aspirational).

Closer:
- Funding: `We're hiring across engineering, design, and beyond. → {careers URL}`
- Year-end: `Keep sketching, keep shipping.`

## `x_thread.md` — 3–4 tweet thread (this is the one signal type that warrants extending past 2 tweets)

**Tweet 1:**
```
We're excited to announce our {$X} Series {Letter} led by @{LeadInvestor} with continued support from @{Investor2}, @{Investor3} + our incredible angel investors.

At Vizcom, we believe in amplifying the human spark behind every object in the world.

Thank you to the [continues...]
```

**Tweet 2:** Thank-you continues — community first, team second.
**Tweet 3:** What this enables — depth in core domains (automotive, footwear, apparel, consumer goods).
**Tweet 4 (optional):** Hiring link or community ask.

## `pr_pitch.md`

**Subject:** `Vizcom raises {$X} Series {Letter}`

**Three sentences:**
1. `Vizcom, the AI design platform used by ~700,000 designers and 150+ companies including {3 named customers}, has raised {$X} in Series {Letter} funding led by {LeadInvestor}.`
2. `The round, with continued participation from {prior investors}, will fund Vizcom's expansion from visualization into the broader creative workflow across automotive, footwear, apparel, and consumer goods.`
3. `Jordan Taylor, CEO and co-founder, is available for comment; full announcement at {blog URL}.`

**Contact:** Jordan Taylor for funding; Sophia Silver for year-in-review; hiring lead for team-expansion. (See `core_rules.md` Section 9.)

## `summary.md` notes

- PR pitch goes out the **evening before** for a morning embargo. Blog goes live at the embargo time, then LinkedIn / X / Instagram in sequence.
- Voice self-check: founder-letter tone, plural "we," no company-emotion ("so excited"), no exclamation points outside cleared quotes.
